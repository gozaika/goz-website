Section I — SVG Logo Analysis
Both uploaded SVG files are clean vector assets with zero raster content embedded. They are production-ready subject to two colour corrections.
1.1  flame.svg — App Icon / BAM Bag Mark
A 512×512 SVG with viewBox cropped to the flame region (88 64 320 320). The flame silhouette is the tabler-icons open path, filled with orange. A mask cuts the text “BAM” as negative space through the flame body.
Strengths
Fully clean vector — no embedded rasters
BAM text cut as mask is clever at 512px+ (icon scale)
Flame shape is crisp and scalable to any size
Mask technique means BAM is invisible at small sizes (subtle)
Issues to Fix
Colour #EF8400 (amber) ≠ brand spec #FF6B35 (coral-orange)
Open path fill is not Z-closed — works but is non-standard
BAM stroke paths use Q curves not a real font — acceptable
No background rectangle for app store square format
1.2  gozaika.svg — Horizontal Wordmark
A 600×153px horizontal wordmark. Each letter g, Z, a, i, k, a is a standalone vector path with per-letter translate+scale transforms. The flame from flame.svg is composited into the ‘o’ position, so the word reads g[flame]Zaika = goZaika. Truly vector — no rasters whatsoever.
Strengths
Fully clean vector — confirmed no rasters
Letter paths are font-independent (no Google Fonts dependency)
Flame-as-‘o’ is a clever brand device
BAM mask in flame is subtle at wordmark scale (correct)
Ready for SVG export, React component, Framer, Canva
Issues to Fix
Orange colour: #EF8400 should be corrected to #FF6B35
Green colour: #37674E should be corrected to #1A5C38
No white variant exists yet (needed for dark backgrounds)
No horizontal safe-area padding in viewBox
1.3  Colour Correction Instructions
Open both SVG files in a text editor (VS Code) and do a find-and-replace:
#EF8400  →  #FF6B35   (Saffron Flame — all orange elements)
#37674E  →  #1A5C38   (Forest Deep — all green elements)
After correction save as gozaika-logo-color.svg and gozaika-logo-white.svg (replace all colour values with #FFFFFF for the white variant).
1.4  White Variant (gozaika-logo-white.svg)
For the white wordmark: open gozaika.svg, replace #FF6B35 with #FFFFFF and #1A5C38 with #FFFFFF. This creates a single-colour white mark for dark nav bars, footers, and dark-mode contexts.
1.5  App Icon (gozaika-app-icon.svg)
Wrap flame.svg in a Forest Green (#1A5C38) rounded-rectangle background with rx="80" for iOS/Android safe corners. Export as 1024×1024 PNG for app stores. The BAM text mask will be visible and meaningful at this size.

AGREED — Next.js/React is the right call for goZaika’s situation
You launch a public URL in 1–2 weeks at zero cost
Vercel free tier gives you gozaika.vercel.app immediately (no domain needed)

Better SEO than Framer out of the box (SSG + metadata API)
Framer CMS at ₸4,000/month was unnecessary cost for a pre-revenue startup
AI-assisted coding (Cursor + Claude) makes Next.js as fast as Framer for a founder

6.5  FAQ Copy
Consumer FAQs:
What exactly is a BAM Bag?  —  A BAM Bag (Big Aayega Maza) is a chef-curated to-go selection from a partner restaurant. The contents are a surprise, but all allergens are always disclosed before you buy.
How do I know if the food is safe?  —  Every partner restaurant is FSSAI-licensed. Every listing declares all 14 major allergens (FSSAI standard). You know what’s in it — just not the exact dish.
Is this leftover food?  —  No. Partner restaurants decide exactly what goes into each BAM Bag, including freshly prepared items they choose to release on their terms. We never describe any bag as “leftover” and do not accept bags that are not kitchen-safe.
What is the pickup process?  —  You receive a QR code on purchase. Show it at the restaurant counter during the stated pickup window. No delivery, no waiting.
Can I cancel or get a refund?  —  BAM Bags are non-cancellable once the kitchen has been notified (typically at purchase). See our Refund Policy for exceptions.
What is Swaad Club?  —  Our loyalty programme. Earn points on every bag. Redeem for early access, exclusive drops, and partner offers. Launching with the app.
Restaurant FAQs:
Who decides what goes in the bag?  —  You do. goZaika gives you a simple dashboard to create a bag listing: cuisine type, allergens, pickup window, serves count, minimum menu value, and price. The contents are entirely your decision.
What’s the commission structure?  —  0% for your first 30 days. 12% during pilot. 15% standard. Volume pricing at 200+ bags/month. No hidden fees.
Do I need a separate FSSAI licence?  —  goZaika holds a Central FSSAI e-commerce FBO licence. You need a valid State or Central FSSAI licence for your own kitchen (which you already have as a licensed restaurant).
What if a bag goes unsold?  —  You are never charged for unsold bags. You pay commission only on bags that are claimed and picked up.

Section IX — Phase 2: Adding Dynamic Content
When the Node.js API is ready (per goZaika Technology Specification v1.0), swapping static content for live data requires minimal code changes.
9.1  Dynamic Migration Pattern
Every hardcoded data array in Phase 1 has a direct API endpoint in the tech spec. The migration is a straight swap:
Static → Dynamic Swap Table
Hardcoded restaurant cards  →  GET /api/restaurants?city=hyderabad
Hardcoded FAQ array          →  GET /api/faqs
Hardcoded city list          →  GET /api/cities
Form POST to Resend          →  POST /api/waitlist (saves to PostgreSQL)
Static testimonials          →  GET /api/testimonials?approved=true
The Next.js App Router's fetch caching means you can call the API at build time (SSG) or at request time (SSR). For the restaurant list, SSR with a 60-second cache is appropriate for Phase 2.
